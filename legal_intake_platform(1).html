Case Studies & Federal Department Intake Tips

Federal Agency Filing Tips

When dealing with federal agencies, timing and proper documentation are critical. Here are essential tips for common federal department filings:

üèõÔ∏è EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission)
  • Deadline: 180 days from incident (300 days in deferral states)
  • Key Tip: File as soon as possible - don't wait until deadline
  • Documentation: Keep detailed records of incidents, witnesses, and communications
  • Dual Filing: Always be prepared to file both EEOC and private lawsuit
  • Right to Sue: You can request immediately or wait for EEOC investigation
‚öñÔ∏è NLRB (National Labor Relations Board)
  • Deadline: 6 months from unfair labor practice
  • Key Tip: Document all communications about union activities
  • Protection: Covers most private sector employees (not supervisors)
  • Dual Filing: Can pursue both NLRB and civil remedies
  • Retaliation: File immediately if terminated for union activity
ü¶∫ OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration)
  • Deadline: 30 days for discrimination complaints
  • Key Tip: Report safety violations immediately to employer first
  • Protection: Whistleblower protection for safety reporting
  • Documentation: Photos, witness statements, medical records
  • Dual Filing: Can file with OSHA and pursue workers' comp/civil claims
üöÇ NMB (National Mediation Board)
  • Coverage: Railroad and airline employees only
  • Key Tip: Railway Labor Act has different procedures than NLRA
  • Process: Mediation first, then arbitration
  • Strikes: Very limited - must follow specific procedures
  • Representation: Different rules for union elections

Case Study: Successful EEOC Dual Filing

Situation: Employee experienced racial discrimination and was terminated after complaining.

Strategy: Filed EEOC charge immediately, requested right-to-sue letter after 180 days, filed federal lawsuit while maintaining EEOC case.

Outcome: Secured $150,000 settlement through private litigation while EEOC case was still pending.

Key Lesson: Dual filing strategy maximized leverage and options.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Reminders

  • Be Timely: Missing deadlines can bar your claims forever
  • Document Everything: Contemporaneous records are crucial
  • Dual File When Possible: Keep all options open
  • Seek Counsel Early: Attorneys can help navigate complex procedures
  • Know Your Rights: Retaliation for filing is illegal

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is my information secure?

A: Yes. All information is encrypted with enterprise-grade security and automatically destroyed after 90 days. We comply with GDPR and HIPAA standards.

Q: How much does this service cost?

A: Our platform is free for clients. We earn revenue through lead generation fees paid by law firms when they accept cases.

Q: How quickly will I hear from an attorney?

A: Most clients hear from qualified attorneys within 24-48 hours. Urgent matters (like approaching deadlines) are prioritized.

Q: Can I use voice input if English isn't my first language?

A: Yes! Our AI supports multiple accents and dialects. Spanish support is currently in beta, with more languages coming soon.

Q: What if I have a federal agency deadline?

A: Our AI automatically detects potential federal agency matters (EEOC, NLRB, OSHA, NMB) and flags them for urgent attorney review.

Q: Can I edit my intake information after submitting?

A: Yes, during the voice interview process, you can update and clarify any information. All changes are tracked for attorney review.
